This blog publishes **analysis grounded in data, public documents, and transparent methods**. The aim is not to write opinion pieces, but to produce clear, disciplined posts that help readers understand patterns in trade, industrial exposure, strategic inputs, and resilience.

## Focus areas

- Trade dependencies and supplier concentration
- Reverse dependencies and upstream leverage
- Energy security and industrial resilience
- Economic security and strategic exposure
- Methods, data sources, and replication notes

## Approach

Each post should try to answer three questions:

1. What is the analytical question?
2. What data and method are being used?
3. What can reasonably be concluded — and what cannot?

## Why this site exists

This site is also a portfolio of applied research. It is meant to show the ability to:

- define tractable analytical questions
- work with trade and firm-level data where available
- replicate public methodologies
- communicate clearly and carefully
